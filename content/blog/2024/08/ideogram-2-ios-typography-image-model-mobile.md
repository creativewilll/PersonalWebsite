# Ideogram 2.0 + iOS App: The Typography-Focused Image Model Goes Mobile

**The take:** Last week Ideogram shipped 2.0 as a full platform drop—not just a weight bump. The company is packaging a frontier text-to-image model with an iOS client, a searchable public gallery, and a developer API beta at the same moment. For anyone who lives in mockups, ads, and packaging, the story is not only prettier pixels; it is legible type, controllable styles, and a path to automate creative output without fighting the model on spelling.

## What actually launched (and where)

Ideogram’s own announcement lines up the pieces clearly: **Ideogram 2.0** is available on the web and in a **new iOS app**, with **premium features** gated behind subscriptions. The same release introduces a **public beta of the Ideogram API**, **Ideogram Search** over community images, and upgraded **Describe** and **Magic Prompt** tooling. Android is explicitly **planned for later**—so this week, mobile means iPhone first.

| Surface | What you get this week |
|--------|-------------------------|
| Web + iOS | Core 2.0 generation, subscription upsells |
| Ideogram Search | Text search across a large public corpus of prior renders |
| API (beta) | Programmatic access; pricing framed as competitive vs. DALL·E 3 / Flux Pro |
| Prompt tools | Describe (image → detailed prompt) + Magic Prompt iterations |

Ideogram says users produced **more than one billion** images in year one—that corpus is now **searchable** for inspiration.

## Why typography still defines Ideogram’s wedge

Ideogram’s pitch is still **text that reads like text**. The **Design** style targets long, stylized lettering for posters, social, and print-on-demand—work where other models still botch spelling. **Realistic** advertises convincing photos; **3D** and **Anime** round out presets. Aspect ratios include **3:1** and **1:3** for banners without manual stitching.

**Color palette control** locks brand tones—a control API buyers actually invoice around.

## Models, benchmarks, and what to verify yourself

Ideogram says 2.0 was **trained from scratch** and posts **human evaluation** charts favoring it over **Flux Pro** and **DALL·E 3**—directional, self-reported, worth re-running on your prompts. **API pricing** is billed as **under DALL·E 3** and **near Flux Pro**. If latency and retries hold, that is viable for **n8n/Make**, in-app renders, and dynamic creative—the same automation layer as LLMs.

## iOS: distribution, not a toy

The **iOS app** is a friction play: iterate away from the desk and upsell subscriptions. The open question late August is whether teams adopt it versus **native OS photo AI**—compare [Made by Google 2024: Pixel 9 + Gemini](/blog/made-by-google-2024-pixel-9-gemini-pixel-studio).

## Trust is now part of the SKU

Coverage including *PetaPixel* flagged that **photorealistic** outputs could depict **recognizable public figures** in ways stricter stacks block. Ideogram’s own copy touts photos that “pass as real.” **Better type plus better realism** moves provenance, disclosure, and abuse response from legal footnotes to product design. Builders need **labels, limits, audits, review** before shipping customer-facing APIs.

## Bottom line + stack placement

**Midjourney** still wins cult aesthetics; **Stable Diffusion** wins self-host; **ChatGPT-side** tooling wins distribution for OpenAI. Ideogram bundles **typography, palette steering, mobile, and API**—unusual as a package.
