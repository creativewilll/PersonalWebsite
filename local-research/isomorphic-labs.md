### Overview of Isomorphic Labs
Isomorphic Labs is a London-based AI company founded in 2021 by Demis Hassabis (also CEO of DeepMind) as a spinout from Alphabet's DeepMind. It focuses on accelerating drug discovery using advanced AI models, building directly on DeepMind's Nobel Prize-winning **AlphaFold** technology (AlphaFold 2 awarded Chemistry Nobel in 2024 to Hassabis and John Jumper). The company's mission is ambitious: to "solve all disease" by creating AI-driven processes that could produce dozens of drugs annually, targeting "undruggable" conditions like cancer, Alzheimer’s, and protein-misfolding diseases (e.g., Parkinson’s, muscular dystrophy).

### Key Milestones and Funding
- **Founding and Early Funding**: Incorporated Feb 2021, publicly announced Nov 2021. Initially funded by Alphabet's "Other Bets."
- **AlphaFold 3 Release (May 2024)**: Co-developed with DeepMind, this free (for non-commercial research) model predicts protein structures and interactions with drugs, ligands, antibodies, and more. Trained on ~100,000 proteins, it significantly speeds up early drug design by modeling molecular interactions.
- **Series A (March 2025)**: Raised $600M led by Thrive Capital, with Google Ventures. Funds advance AI drug-design engine and pipeline to clinical trials. Valuation undisclosed.
- **Drug Design Engine (Feb 2026)**: Proprietary upgrade doubling AlphaFold 3's performance on protein-ligand benchmarks. Excels in binding-affinity predictions (faster/cheaper than physics-based methods) and identifying new protein binding pockets from amino acid sequences alone.

### Technology and Approach
Isomorphic's **Drug Design Engine** integrates:
- Updated protein prediction (beyond AlphaFold).
- Models for peptides, molecular glues, antibodies.
- Data sources: Protein Data Bank, UK Biobank, licensed/commercial/internal/partner datasets.

This compresses years of trial-and-error preclinical work into minutes/digital simulations, focusing on precise molecular interactions. Unlike traditional biotech (1-2 drugs/lifetime), Isomorphic aims for high-volume output (dozens/year) via AI "pattern recognition."

**Commercial Applications**:
- **Pipeline Focus**: Oncology drugs first. As of early 2026, preparing to dose first patients in clinical trials (per president Colin Murdoch). "Staffing up" for trials; expanding beyond oncology using AlphaFold's versatility.
- **Partnerships/Progress**: No public deals detailed, but engine targets intractable diseases. Emphasizes "structure-based" design to predict drug-target fits, reducing failure rates.
- **Beyond AlphaFold**: Extends to full drug-design pipeline, generative AI for novel molecules, and performance prediction.

Hassabis envisions AI making drug discovery "magical" yet standard in 10-20 years, though skeptics note AI's history of overpromising in biotech.
