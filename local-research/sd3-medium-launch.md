### Stable Diffusion 3 Medium Launch Overview
Stable Diffusion 3 Medium (SD3 Medium) was officially launched by **Stability AI on June 12, 2024** (with some sources referencing June 13 coverage). It's positioned as their "most sophisticated text-to-image open model yet," featuring a Diffusion Transformer (DiT) architecture for improved photorealism, better handling of complex prompts, accurate text rendering (e.g., no spelling errors), and reduced artifacts in hands/faces. The model is resource-efficient (optimized for consumer GPUs like Nvidia RTX with TensorRT, offering up to 50% performance boost) and has a smaller parameter count compared to larger SD3 variants (e.g., SD3 Large or Ultra).

- **Key Capabilities** (per Stability AI):
  - High prompt adherence and style flexibility.
  - Runs on laptops/consumer PCs due to lower VRAM needs.
  - Available via API (powered by Fireworks AI), Stable Assistant (free 3-day trial), and Stable Artisan on Discord.

Model weights are downloadable from Stability AI's site for community use.

### Licensing: Stability Community License
SD3 Medium is released under the **Stability Community License** (sometimes referenced as "Stability Non-Commercial Research Community License" in early announcements). This is **not fully open-source** like prior models (e.g., SD 1.5 under CreativeML Open RAIL-M), marking a shift.

- **Core Permissions**:
  - Allows use by "professional artists, designers, developers, and AI enthusiasts" for building and experimentation.
  - Free for non-commercial research and community projects.

- **Commercial Restrictions** (Source of Controversy):
  - **Prohibits large-scale commercial use** without an Enterprise License.
  - Enterprises and high-volume commercial users must **contact Stability AI for paid licensing**.
  - Vague thresholds (e.g., no explicit revenue/usage limits defined publicly), leading to uncertainty.

This license supports Stability AI's goal of "keeping AI open and accessible" while funding development, but it's restrictive compared to permissive open-source licenses.
