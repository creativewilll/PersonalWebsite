### ChatGPT Search vs. SearchGPT Prototype: Comparison as of December 2024

SearchGPT was initially launched by OpenAI in July 2024 as a **temporary standalone prototype** for testing AI-powered search features. It was designed to deliver fast, timely answers by combining OpenAI's AI models (like a fine-tuned GPT-4o) with real-time web data, emphasizing clear sources and conversational responses. By October 2024, its core capabilities were **integrated directly into ChatGPT**, rebranded as **ChatGPT Search**. This merger made SearchGPT obsolete as a separate tool, with ChatGPT Search becoming the active evolution available to users.

In December 2024, ChatGPT Search had rolled out more widely (e.g., to additional ChatGPT users per TechCrunch reporting on Dec 16), building on the prototype's foundation while addressing limitations. Below is a detailed comparison focused on **features, sources, citations, news, sports, and weather** handling, based on contemporaneous reports from Stackmatix, PageOnePower, SearchEngineJournal, OpenAI, TechCrunch, and BrightEdge.

#### Technical Differences (Query Pipeline)
- **SearchGPT Prototype**: Focused on prototype feedback—web index (via Bing-like live data) + GPT-4 synthesis for concise answers. Manual-like triggering.
- **ChatGPT Search**: Advanced pipeline:
  1. **Query Analysis**: Intent classification, temporal checks, entity recognition → decides web trigger.
  2. **Web Execution**: Multi-source queries, news prioritization.
  3. **Generation**: Conversational output with inline citations, follow-ups, shopping (active by 2025-26).

#### Performance Notes from Dec 2024 Tests (SearchEngineJournal)
- ChatGPT Search often more accurate than Google/Bing on niche queries.
- Bing ranking influences but doesn't guarantee citations.
- Responses vary even from same account; UTM tracking on links.
